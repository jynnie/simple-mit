SIMPLE

a Super Intuitive Method of Programmatically Listing Emails

by kyc, michaelx, & runpeng

Want to send to a mailing list but exclude someone?

Now, for the next birthday email or surprise party, just do one of the following:

  • {safetythird}-{michaelx}@simple.mit.edu: send to everyone in safetythird except michaelx.
  • safetythird--kyc2915@simple.mit.edu: note that without curly braces, you need two minus signs!
  • safetythird-minus-dyhwong@simple.mit.edu: the verbose way.
  • {safetythird}-{dyhwong}.mitonly@simple.mit.edu: don't worry about inadvertently sending to an alternate Gmail on the list and ruining the surprise -- filter for '@mit' addresses only!

Important: you must address your email to simple.mit.edu, not mit.edu.

You can use this syntax for both a list minus a kerberos, as well as a list minus another list. You can even exclude multiple email addresses and lists!

But wait! SIMPLE can do more. Keep reading to find out what else you can do with SIMPLE.

Syntax Details

Currently three operations are supported: minus (-), plus (+) and intersect (&). An example for syntax is best shown with a table:
Braces notation Quick notation Verbose notation
minus (everyone except Tom Riddle): {safetythird}-{tomriddle}@simple.mit.edu safetythird--tomriddle@simple.mit.edu safetythird-minus-tomriddle@simple.mit.edu
plus (BC and Next): {bc-talk}+{next-forum}@simple.mit.edu bc-talk++next-forum@simple.mit.edu bc-talk-plus-next-forum@simple.mit.edu
intersect (safetythird 2017s): {safetythird}&{mit2017}@simple.mit.edu safetythird&&mit2017@simple.mit.edu safetythird-intersect-mit2017@simple.mit.edu

You can chain operations together, and they will be evaluated from left to right. For example, safetythird--tomriddle++bc-talk&&mit2017@simple.mit.edu will take the members of safetythird, remove Tom Riddle, add everyone from BC, and finally from those emails filter out only the members of the class of 2017.

The braces notation also supports nested expressions. For example, {safetythird}-{{{tomriddle}+{bc-talk}}&mit2017}@simple.mit.edu will first take Tom Riddle, add the members of bc-talk, and from those take only the members of mit2017. Then your email will be sent to everyone in safetythird not in that list.

Dry Runs

You may be unsure about whether a given email will actually be parsed in the same way that you think it will. If you have doubts, you can always submit a dry run email to SIMPLE containing the email that you want to test. Just append '.dryrun' to the end of the email, before the '@' sign:

  • send to {safetythird}-{michaelx}.dryrun@simple.mit.edu

The above email will send you a message specifying how SIMPLE will parse the email, without actually sending the email to anyone.

FAQ

  • Can I just type @mit.edu, instead of @simple.mit.edu?

    Sorry, we run a separate email server that parses the emails, so you must go through SIMPLE.

  • My emails didn't send to anyone.

    You can first try to submit a dry run email to see if the SIMPLE server is running correctly. Also, verify that you did not receive an error message email, possibly in your spam folder.

  • Can I exclude multiple people?

    Yes, just send to something of the form {list}-{user1}-{user2}@simple.mit.edu.

  • My stealthy friend has their non-MIT (e.g. Gmail) address on a mailing list. How can I still exclude them?

    Fear not! Just append '.mitonly' to the end of the email address, before the '@' sign. For example, sending to {safetythird}-{dyhwong}.mitonly@simple.mit.edu will send only to valid '@mit' addresses on safetythird, except 'dyhwong@mit.edu'

    Can I put SIMPLE emails in mailing lists?

    Absolutely. However, MIT's mailing list server does not seem to handle the curly braces in {list}-{user}@simple.mit.edu correctly, so please use either quick notation (list--user@simple.mit.edu) or verbose notation (list-minus-user@simple.mit.edu) instead when using SIMPLE emails in mailing lists.

  • Can I cc or bcc a SIMPLE email?

    Yes, a SIMPLE email is just like a normal email address.

  • Can I send to a hidden list?

    Yes, provided you have the permissions to view the members of the list. If so, send your email as you normally would, through SIMPLE. SIMPLE will then send you an activation link, which will ask you to login through Webathena and complete the sending process.

  • I have a question that is not on this list.

    Please ask us at admin@simple.mit.edu.

Feature Requests / Bug Fixes

  • 4/27/2017: Fixed a bug where dryrun results for using the '.mitonly' flag were not accurate.

  • 4/21/2017: Implemented feature to exclude all non-MIT email addresses (i.e. Gmail, Yahoo, etc.) from a mailing list. Just add '.mitonly' right before the '@' sign in the email address.

  • 3/15/2015: Switched to the Webathena interface to more safely allow administrators of hidden lists to send list members to SIMPLE.

  • 3/6/2015: Implemented a more lenient email parser to fix several common mistakes when sending to SIMPLE email addresses.

  • 3/2/2015: Fixed a bug where SIMPLE corrupts unicode-encoded emails.

  • 1/17/2014: We have added a user-friendly error when the MIT directory is unavailable.

  • 11/6/2014: Added a script to allow administrators of hidden lists to send their list members to SIMPLE, thereby allowing SIMPLE to send to these lists.

  • 12/17/2013: Fixed a bug when sending to @ATHENA.MIT.EDU emails.

  • 12/15/2013: SIMPLE does not support mailman lists, but sometimes when users use a mailman list, an uninformative error about empty intersections shows up. We have fixed this error message to clarify that the error is the usage of the mailman list.

  • 12/14/2013: We discovered a bug where on days that SIMPLE is being used too much, some emails are not sent. We have fixed this problem.

  • 11/29/2013: We have increased the recipient limit (maximum number of recipients each email can be sent to) to 2000, to accomodate large lists like ec-discuss.

  • 11/15/2013: We have added more detail to the dryrun results email, such as some example emails that your message will be sent to or not be sent to.

  • 11/14/2013: For fun, we have implemented the xor function for braces notation only, so you can now send to {list1}^{list2}@simple.mit.edu.

  • 11/14/2013: We have added nested expressions; see the paragraph in "Syntax Details" for more information.
